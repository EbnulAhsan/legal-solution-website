What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet is over 5 years old, has cracks, or has been in a crash, it's time to replace it. Worn-out padding, loose fit, or faded straps are also signs you need a new one.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If your bike has been in storage, check the tires, brakes, and chain before riding. Make sure there’s no rust, loose parts, or flat tires for a safe ride.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always follow traffic rules, signals, and ride in the same direction as traffic. Wear a helmet, use lights at night, and stay alert to your surroundings.
